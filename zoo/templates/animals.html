Meet Our Animals

Riget Zoo Adventures is home to an incredible variety of animals from all corners of the globe. From the fierce and majestic to the small and curious, each species has its own unique story to tell.

Animal Highlights

  • Big Cats: Marvel at our lions, tigers, and leopards.
  • Primates: Meet our playful chimpanzees and lemurs.
  • Reptiles: Discover snakes, lizards, and crocodiles in our reptile house.
  • Birds: Admire colourful parrots, flamingos, and majestic eagles.

Visit our zoo and learn about the habitats, behaviours, and conservation efforts for each of these amazing creatures. Every visit helps us continue to provide exceptional care for our animals.

Explore Our Animal Gallery

Big Cats

Our big cat exhibit showcases the majestic lions, tigers, and leopards that call Riget Zoo home. These powerful predators are an integral part of the animal kingdom. Each species has unique behaviours, from the territorial pride of lions to the solitary nature of tigers. The conservation of big cats is crucial, as many species are endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.

Primates

Our primate family includes playful chimpanzees, curious lemurs, and intelligent orangutans. These incredible creatures share many similarities with humans, making them both fascinating and relatable. Primates are social animals, living in tight-knit groups. Their intelligence is astounding, and many can use tools, solve problems, and communicate with one another in complex ways.

Reptiles

Our reptile house is home to a variety of fascinating creatures including snakes, lizards, and crocodiles. These cold-blooded animals have adapted to diverse environments, from desert landscapes to rainforests. Some, like the chameleons, are known for their ability to change colour, while others, like the crocodiles, are ancient survivors with a history dating back millions of years.

Birds

The bird section of Riget Zoo is filled with stunning species like colourful parrots, elegant flamingos, and majestic eagles. Birds are known for their ability to fly, but some species, like penguins, have adapted to life on the ground or in water. Many birds are migratory, traveling thousands of miles each year, while others have become experts at navigating their local habitats.
